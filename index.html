About

An honest number for what you wear

Suschecker is an independent tool that scores the environmental and ethical footprint of clothing — before you buy it. Paste a product link and get a clear 0–100 SustainScore in under a minute.

Why we built it

Fashion is one of the most polluting industries on earth, yet the “sustainable” and “eco” labels stitched onto product pages are mostly unverifiable marketing. We wanted a single honest number — grounded in real life-cycle data, not brand promises — that anyone can read at a glance.

What it does

We read what a product page actually says — materials, country of manufacture, certifications and circularity signals — and combine it with a full 16-category life-cycle assessment to produce a grade from A to F, plus a breakdown of exactly why.

Independent by design

Brands can’t pay to raise their score. Every scan uses the same public data and the same model. When we can’t verify a claim, we say so rather than giving credit for it.

Methodology

How the score is built

Every product gets a single 0–100 SustainScore, built from four weighted pillars and adjusted by penalties and transparency boosts. Here’s exactly what goes into it.

The four pillars

Material Footprint
Up to 30 pts

The impact of the fibres themselves — how the raw materials are grown or produced. Natural, recycled and certified fibres score higher; virgin synthetics and blends score lower.

Product Circularity
Up to 20 pts

Whether the product is built to last and stay out of landfill — repair services, resale and take-back schemes, and lifetime guarantees.

Supply Chain & Labour
Up to 20 pts

Labour conditions and transparency — named factories, audit reports, published supplier lists and credible third-party certifications.

Environmental Cost
Up to 15 pts

A modelled cradle-to-gate environmental cost from a full 16-category life-cycle assessment (EF 3.1, via Ecobalyse).

Penalties & boosts

Fast-fashion business models, fibre blends that can’t be recycled, and high synthetic content reduce the score. Verified circularity and supply-chain transparency add to it. The breakdown on every result shows which adjustments were applied.

The Impact Profile

Beyond the headline score, every result shows where a product’s environmental cost actually lands across 16 impact categories — climate, toxicity, resources and water — normalised and weighted under the EU’s EF 3.1 method, and compared against a typical item in the same category.

Data Confidence

Some product pages are richer than others. When key details are missing we estimate from category and material defaults, and flag the result as lower-confidence — so you always know how much to trust it.

FAQ

Questions, answered

Where does the data come from?

From the public product page you submit plus the brand’s own published information, combined with the Ecobalyse life-cycle database. We don’t treat marketing claims as fact unless they’re verifiable.

How accurate are the scores?

They’re a well-grounded estimate, not a lab measurement. The score reflects what can be verified from available data; where information is missing we flag the result as lower-confidence.

What is the “environmental cost”?

A single modelled figure (in mPt) summarising a product’s impact across 16 categories — from climate change to water use — using the EU’s EF 3.1 method.

Why did a product I like score low?

Often it’s a lack of verifiable information, a fibre blend that can’t be recycled, or a high-impact material — not necessarily bad intent. Open the score breakdown to see exactly why.

Can brands pay to improve their score?

No. Scoring is automatic and identical for every product. There’s no paid placement and no way to opt out.

How do I scan a product?

Paste the product URL on the home page. The scan usually takes 30–60 seconds and the result appears right there, then joins the dashboard.

What does it cover?

Clothing and apparel for now. Other categories may follow.
